About Me

I've been a lot of things: a landscaper, a student, an overnight hotel manager, a writer, a volunteer coordinator, a warehouse manager, a marketing professional, a James Dean impersonater - the list goes on. Nothing has sparked my imagination and interest quite like coding.

I took my first CS class in Python during my last semester of college (I needed to fulfill a lab credit and I didn't want to disect things). It ended up being my favorite class in college. It wasn't until last year, though, that a friend sold me on the idea that I could keep pursuing that dream through Dev Bootcamp, a 9-week hurricane of training in Ruby, Rails, JavaScript, TDD, Git, Databases, learning acceleration techniques, and so much more.

I was hooked. I have been ever since and I don't see myself stopping any time soon.

Outside of coding, I love cooking (especially with America's Test Kitchen - cooking and science belong together), hiking, making music, reading, and writing short stories.
