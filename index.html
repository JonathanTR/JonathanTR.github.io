Hi,

My name is Jonathan Turnbull-Reilly and I love coding. When I say that I love coding, I mean that wading into a complex problem without an apparent solution and breaking it down into simple, modular pieces, and then solving and reassembling those pieces lights up the dopamine centers in my brain like a Christmas tree. I recently had the time of my life completing Dev Bootcamp, a web development training program where I spent over 900 hours in 9 weeks accelerating my rate of learning and building a solid foundation in the Rails stack.

You should know that I care about people, a lot.

In my opinion, coding is primarily a tool for connecting and communicating with other people. That shapes the ideals that I strive for as a programmer:

  • TDD: I believe in writing code that is easily maintainable and preserves the sanity of my fellow programmers as well as my future self.
  • Agile Development: I believe in start small, deploying early, and creating features users actually want.
  • Modular Design: Pay strict attention to imputs and outputs. Wherever possible, write code that fits together like lengths of garden hose.
