About Me

Hi there! I’m Jonathan. I’m a software engineer living and working in Boston, Massachusetts. I've spent the last three years working with a team of stellar engineers, designers, and product managers to deliver a beautiful product experience for Wistia.

What I’ve worked on

I worked on a ton of projects I was proud of at Wistia. While I was there, I

  • overhauled a customer bandwidth tracker that accounted for up to 30% of company revenue, making it transparent, tested, and reliable. It's been scaling without issue for over two years.
  • developed an internal style library so that future developers could use ready-made, flexible UI elements to speed up their projects.
  • helped plan and execute the migration of several of our core features (our video player and customizer) into consistent, tested, React/Redux applications.
  • took our video captioning system multi-lingual, adding support for more than 80 languages to our player, along with a tool to manage, edit, and order transcriptions.
  • provided engineering support and product advisement to a cross-functional team focused on increasing customer adoption rates.

In addition to my engineering work, I also:

  • created technical challenges, interviewed, and help to decide on engineering candidates, many of whom became close collaborators during my time there.
  • co-founded and co-chaired Diverstia, Wistia's employee-led diversity and inclusion initiative.

Side Projects

I also volunteer with the Progressive Coders Network, a nationwide group of programmers, designers, and others who work together to support progressive advocacy initiatives around the United States. Most recently, I've been bootstrapping a call tool (React frontend, Rails API backend, deployed with Docker) for a canvassing group called Knock Every Door.

I also created Typedash to motivate myself and some friends to sit down and just write (I write short stories in my spare time). One friend said of Typedash, and I quote, "I finished my MFA thesis on it." So there's that.

Things I'm good at

Technical: I started out as a Ruby developer, but the front end is where I really find my flow. Still, I'm always down to jump on a back end challenge and more recently I've been dipping my toes into the DevOps waters for some of my volunteer projects.

Non-technical: I love direct collaboration and I especially dig pair programming. I also really enjoy helping to develop and refine product decisions from an engineering perspective.
